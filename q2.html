Tim Berners-Lee and the World Wide Web

Tim Berners-Lee is known for inventing the World Wide Web, which changed the way we access and share information over the internet. He invented it in 1989 while working at CERN but its impact on the world is still greatly present and growing to this day.

The Three Key Parts of the Invention

    HTML (Hypertext Markup Language): Used to structure content on the web, allowing it to be displayed in browsers.

    URL (Uniform Resource Locator): A standardized system for addressing resources on the web, such as web pages or files.

    HTTP (Hypertext Transfer Protocol): A protocol for transmitting data over the internet, enabling communication between web browsers and servers.
